Description

The city spreads across enormous terraces of pale grey stone beneath towering cliffs scarred by centuries of quarrying. Massive stairways, carved plazas, and broad processional roads divide districts filled with heavy dwarven and giant architecture, sprawling residential quarters, and monuments so old many citizens no longer remember who first built them. Everywhere the sound of stone echoes through the air — chisels striking marble, cranes hauling blocks through the quarry pits, water channels grinding cutting wheels, and distant hammering from deep beneath the mountain.

Unlike many dwarven cities, Stonehold feels open. Sunlight reaches much of the city through vast plazas and exposed terraces overlooking the valleys below. Markets bustle beneath carved colonnades while great statues and obelisks line roads leading toward the central Great Hearth Hall. Aqueducts and stone-cutting channels carry cold mountain water through workshops where masons shape blocks for temples, bridges, keeps, and monuments exported across the kingdoms. Even the poorer districts possess a sense of permanence, their homes built from fitted stone meant to endure for generations.

Yet beneath the proud civic grandeur lies the deeper mountain. Mine entrances and tunnel gates disappear into darkness near the northern reaches of the city, guarded by ancient watchposts and monastery bells echoing from higher ridges. Citizens speak casually about the lower workings and old quarry depths, but visitors quickly notice how often conversations stop when the deepest tunnels are mentioned. Stonehold is a city devoted to shaping stone — but also one that understands the mountain is always shaping them in return.

Key Factions

Hearth Circle - Stonehold's governing council of elected master masons. Members are judged as much by the works they leave behind as by the policies they enact.
The Conserve - The city's most influential coalition of masons, architects, sculptors and engineers. The Conserve oversees major civic projects and often determines which works will shape Stonehold for generations.
Quarrymasters Accord - The powerful organisation responsible for Stonehold's quarries, mining operations and stone exports. Their control over the city's raw materials grants them considerable political influence.
Greymantles - Stonehold's city guard and keepers of civic order. Known for their professionalism and restraint, they are often entrusted with protecting monuments, archives and public works as much as citizens.
Monks of the hearthforge -A monastic order dedicated to preserving traditional crafts, techniques and records. Their ceramic tablets, kiln-fired archives and instructional works can be found throughout Stonehold.
The Memory Keepers - A respected order of archivists, historians and stonewrights responsible for preserving Stonehold's records, inscriptions and civic memory. They oversee the Chiselvault archives, maintain the city's memory walls and ensure that important achievements are remembered long after their creators are gone.
The Rising Hands - A city-wide fellowship supporting apprentices across Stonehold's many crafts. The organisation sponsors training, mediates placements and provides opportunities for promising students to earn recognition through public works and civic projects.

Culture & Traditions

Culture
Public craftsmanship is deeply respected within Stonehold. Massive public works are celebrated with festivals and unveiled during city gatherings.

Apprenticeships are highly competitive and many families spend generations within the same craft halls.

Citizens often earn honorary titles based on their crafts

Memory walls
citizens often carve names of births, deaths and accomplishments into shared public walls rather than paper records alone

The turning of the sundial
Each midsummer, thousands gather around the Stoneclock to witness the Turning of the Sundial. As the sun reaches its highest point, bells ring throughout the city and the names of notable citizens, completed works and recent achievements are formally recorded by the Memory Keepers. Many regard it as Stonehold's most important civic celebration, a reminder that every generation contributes another stone to the city's legacy.
The team of Turners are selected by citywide competition; teams compete in the suntrials including stone setting, endurance runs and speed carving.
Workshops close throughout the city and communal feasts fill plazas, markets and gardens. Families picnic around the Stoneclock while musicians, storytellers and craftsmen gather to celebrate the year's achievements.

The Masterworks
Every half century, Stonehearth builds one new monument as a tribute to a exemplary craftmanship The next Masterwork is due in 7 years, generating political competition, artistic rivalries, and conspiracies. Previous awards have been:
+Torran Spatchfin for the The Apprentice’s Stair – 777 steps carved to “test the endurance of the craft.”
+Mora Stonebraid -legendary mason who carved an entire mural blindfolded.
+Derren & Linette Kilgarich for The Stoneclock

The First Masterwork: Stonehold's oldest Masterwork has no known creator. Records identifying its builders were lost centuries ago, leading some to claim the monument was intentionally left unsigned to prove that legacy matters more than recognition.

The Masterwork ceremony includes:
the raising of the piller: the new pillar is unveiled and every contributor is carved on it
The procession of names: Memory keepers walk through the streets reading the names of every contributor
The Empty Chisel: The final name is only carved at the ceremony by the youngest surviving contributor
In the preceding years the Great Debate rages about what deserves to be remembered, ready for the vote
The week of the unveiling is filled with city feasts, games and dances.

Mark Stones
Citizens carve small personal marks into approved public walls when reaching major milestones.

Histories & Legends

The Found City
Legend claims Stonehold was not built, but uncovered. Early quarry workers digging into the cliffs supposedly revealed ancient roads, walls and foundations already waiting beneath the earth. At the centre stood a single immense stone that resisted every attempt at removal. Taking it as a sign, the settlers expanded upon what they had found rather than building elsewhere. Most dismiss the tale as folklore, though discoveries beneath Stonehold continue to fuel speculation. Some claim the Stone That Refused still rests beneath the Great Hearth Hall.

The Giants of Auldthrone
Ancient stories tell of stone giants who taught the earliest dwarves the secrets of masonry before vanishing into the mountains. Many still leave polished pebbles in hidden caves as offerings.

The sleeping mason
A giant stone figure supposedly buried somewhere beneath the city. Legend says Stonehold sits atop its chest. When earthquakes strike, citizens joke: “The mason turns in his sleep.”

The Weeping Statues
Certain ancient statues in the old districts sometimes develop unexplained moisture resembling tears before deaths of important citizens. Nobody publicly discusses it.

The Returning Chisel
A beautifully crafted chisel displayed within the Great Hearth Hall. According to tradition, it has been lost, stolen and misplaced dozens of times throughout history, yet always finds its way back to Stonehold. No two accounts agree on how.

Nearby Landmarks

The Fallen Pantheon
Enormous figures carved into nearby cliffsides, believed to predate recorded history. Scholars disagree whether they depict forgotten gods, ancient rulers or the giants of Auldthrone.

Skyfall Quarry
A quarry famed for its unusually pure marble. Many of Stonehold's most celebrated monuments began as stone cut from its depths..

The Canaries
Strange runes carved throughout portions of the lower tunnels. Miners claim they emit faint echoes before cave-ins or collapses, earning them the nickname "The Canaries".

The Perfect Bridge
An ancient bridge spanning a deep gorge without mortar, cement or visible supports. Despite centuries of study, no engineer has successfully improved upon its design.

The halfway house
Originally built as a temporary shelter for workers constructing a mountain road, it remained long after the project was finished. Every generation has repaired, expanded or improved it, and nobody knows who technically owns it.

The Spring Peak
A towering mountain overlooking the region, left untouched despite rich stone deposits. Enormous herds of mountain goats, gryphons and other wildlife nest upon its slopes, and generations of Stonehold's leaders have refused permission to quarry it. Reaching the quarries beyond still requires crossing its treacherous passes.

The Footling Grove
An ancient grove maintained by a circle of druids at the foot of the mountains who have long opposed Stonehold's expansion into the surrounding wilderness. Relations remain cordial, though disagreements over quarrying rights and road construction are common.

Great Tomb
A vast stone mausoleum carved into a nearby mountainside. Though many chambers have been explored, sealed doors and unfinished passages continue to fuel rumours of forgotten vaults deeper within.

The Hollow City
Far beneath the Echopits lies a vast abandoned city carved directly into the mountain. Its builders are disputed; some claim duergar, others giants, while a few insist it predates both. Gnoll packs, cave predators and stranger inhabitants are known to occupy its outer districts, though few expeditions have mapped more than a fraction of its streets.

The Shepherd's Crown
A high plateau overlooking the valleys where vast herds are grazed during the summer months. Though peaceful today, ancient standing stones scattered across the grasslands suggest the site once held greater significance.
